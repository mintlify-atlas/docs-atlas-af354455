Comments allow you to provide feedback around specific pieces of content — without switching out of context from GitBook.

Teams can collaborate and review content using comments and discussions, whether on entire pages or specific content blocks.

## Add a comment

You can open the comments panel by clicking on the **Comments** button in the space header.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open comments panel">
    Click the Comments button in the space header
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose comment location">
    - Add a comment here to attach it to the entire page
    - Or hover over a specific block and click the content icon to comment on that block
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tag teammates">
    Tag teammates by typing the `@` symbol followed by their name
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Guests will be able to make comments but they will not be able to see or mention other users in the organization.
</Info>

## Comment threads

You can reply to any comment to start a conversation with your teammates, turning it into a discussion thread.

You can also leave an emoji reaction on any comment by clicking the emoji button on the message or thread you'd like to react to.

## Resolving comments

If you're done working through a comment thread or idea, you can **resolve** a comment at any time.

Resolving a comment will hide it in the interface, but still keep it accessible in the 'Resolved' tab of the space's comments section.

<Note>
Resolved comments remain accessible and can be reopened if needed by navigating to the Resolved tab.
</Note>
